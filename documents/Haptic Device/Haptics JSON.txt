  "project_summary": "Knee instability from ACL injuries affects 80,000-120,000 people annually in the US, with current rehabilitation methods risking muscle atrophy from over-bracing or re-injury from premature free movement. The Knee Instability Correction Device (KICD) bridges this gap using vibrotactile feedback to guide users toward stable knee positions without physically constraining movement.\n\nAs Hardware Lead, I designed and built the complete wearable system: three flex sensors in 3D-printed TPU enclosures detect knee angle across sagittal, abduction, and adduction planes, while six ERM motors arranged in a 40mm-spaced array deliver directional 'tracing' vibrations that intuitively guide the user back to stability. The electronics architecture uses an Arduino Mega controlling the motors through NPN transistor drivers, with ELEGOO Nanos handling sensor communication via connectorized perfboard harnesses for serviceability.\n\nTesting with 30+ subjects demonstrated 100% accuracy for front-bend detection and successful directional perception in the majority of users. Perception studies established optimal motor spacing (40mm for ≥75% discrimination) and characterized intensity perception following a 2nd-order polynomial curve (R²=0.999), informing future PWM modulation strategies.",
