A Novel Accordion-Like Disk Resonator Gyroscope

MEMS Gyroscope Design · Simulation · Fabrication · Testing

🏛️ M2D2 Laboratory, Indian Institute of Science 👤 Prof. G. K. Ananthasuresh 🎤 ICMNSS Conference
3–4× Higher Sensitivity
6771 Hz Drive Frequency
<1.4% Sim-to-Fab Error
46.4% Capacitance Change

Project Overview

Disk Resonator Gyroscopes (DRGs) sense rotation using Coriolis forces acting on a vibrating ring — the same physics behind a Foucault pendulum, just shrunk down to millimeters.

Over the years, designs like the Boeing DRG, Honeycomb DRG, Cobweb DRG, and Gear DRG each pushed sensitivity a little further, from 0.04 deg/sec down to 0.015 deg/sec, but each traded off electrode placement against mechanical performance.

This project asked a simple question: could an entirely different ring geometry — an "accordion-like" pattern — beat the existing field on mechanical sensitivity and noise?

ADRG die showing accordion-style ring pattern

Design & Simulation

I ran finite-element eigenfrequency analysis across five ring topologies — DRG, HDRG, CDRG, GDRG, and my proposed ADRG — each built with 9 rings, an 8mm outer diameter, 10μm in-plane ring width, and 60μm thickness.

DRG Type Eigenfrequency (Hz) Quality Factor (k) Mech. Sensitivity (μm/rad/s) Brownian Noise (rad/s/√Hz)
DRG 12720 103.62 3.83e-6 1.01e-6
HDRG 11306 105.42 4.27e-6 1.29e-6
CDRG 12611 99.99 3.61e-6 1.12e-6
GDRG 14761 91.58 2.76e-6 1.17e-6
ADRG (Ours) 6771 181.55 12.1e-6 1.07e-6

The ADRG's mechanical sensitivity is roughly 3–4× higher than every other topology tested, while holding competitive noise performance.

Capacitance modeling (accounting for fringing fields, not just the textbook parallel-plate formula) backed this up — the ADRG's relative sense capacitance change was the largest of the five designs at 46.4%, versus 14–29% for the others.

Fabrication

The design moved from simulation to silicon at CeNSE (Centre for Nano Science and Engineering).

Individual ADRG chips on glass slides, each with the resonator's accordion-style ring pattern visible under its protective coating — and the completed chip wire-bonded into a 68-pin ceramic package with color-coded leads carrying drive, sense, and common signals to the test board.

Pinout & Test Setup

Electrode Configuration

  • 4 common pins
  • 16 individually addressable outer electrodes for drive and sense
  • Companion accelerometer die with separate X/Y sense and feedback electrode pairs

LDV Testing

  • Drive signal combining AC and DC bias excited the resonator
  • LDV measured out-of-plane velocity at 0° and 180° points on the ring
  • Characterized frequency response under controlled conditions

68-pin ceramic package ready for LDV testing

Results

Under controlled atmospheric conditions (1 atm, 25°C), the fabricated ADRG resonated at approximately 6.8 kHz — within 1.2–1.4% of the FEM-predicted eigenfrequency, confirming the simulation model held up in the real device.
