Problem Statement

  • Need: high-precision inertial sensors for ISRO space navigation
  • Prior art: DRG, Honeycomb (HDRG), Concentric (CDRG), Gear-tooth (GDRG), incremental sensitivity gains, each trading electrode placement against mechanical performance
  • Open question: a ring geometry that raises sensitivity and Q factor without hurting noise performance or manufacturability

Note Why accordion spokes?
Accordion-shaped spokes increase radial compliance while maintaining tangential stiffness. This geometry allows for larger modal displacements under Coriolis forces, directly improving mechanical sensitivity without compromising structural integrity or introducing additional thermoelastic damping paths.

Info More on the pinout
Custom pinout schematics mapped electrode-to-pin for both the gyroscope (16 addressed outer electrodes + 4 common) and the companion accelerometer die on the same package. This allows independent drive/sense control of each electrode pair for differential operation.
Process SOI microfabrication steps
  • Start with SOI wafer (60 µm device layer, 2 µm BOX)
  • Photolithography for device patterning
  • Deep reactive ion etching (DRIE) through device layer
  • HF vapor release of buried oxide
  • Critical point drying to prevent stiction
  • Metal deposition for electrodes and bond pads

Method How the measurement worked
Electrostatic AC+DC drive on opposing electrodes (0°/180° phase); frequency sweeps traced the mechanical response, and the resonance peak gave the eigenfrequency. The LDV measured out-of-plane velocity at multiple points across the resonator to verify mode shapes matched FEM predictions.

Key Finding ADRG performance advantages
The Accordion-like DRG demonstrated 12.1 µm/rad/s mechanical sensitivity — a 2.3× improvement over conventional DRG designs. The accordion spoke geometry achieved 181.6k thermoelastic Q factor while maintaining structural robustness suitable for ISRO's space navigation requirements.
Publication Conference paper details
Sai Pranav Avva, Suman Acharya, Kandula Eswara Sai Kumar, Sudhanshu Shekar, Karthik Raveendranath, and G K Ananthasuresh. "A Novel Accordion-like Disk Resonator Gyroscope." International Conference on Micro, Nano, and Smart Systems (ICMNS), July 11, 2024.

The paper presents a comprehensive comparison of five DRG topologies (DRG, HDRG, CDRG, GDRG, ADRG) across mechanical sensitivity, Q factor, capacitance change, and Brownian noise floor metrics.
Data Topology comparison summary
  • DRG (baseline): 5.2 µm/rad/s sensitivity, 156k Q
  • HDRG: 7.1 µm/rad/s sensitivity, 142k Q
  • CDRG: 6.8 µm/rad/s sensitivity, 168k Q
  • GDRG: 9.4 µm/rad/s sensitivity, 151k Q
  • ADRG (ours): 12.1 µm/rad/s sensitivity, 181.6k Q
